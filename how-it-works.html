ALIGNA is designed to work as a complete system — mat, markings, and movement routines working together to build a consistent, effective home practice.

How to use it

Your practice,
step by step.

The mat removes every source of uncertainty from your home routine. You always know where to start, how to position, and how to move.

1

Lay out the mat — face down

Position the mat on a firm, flat surface. Lie face down for extension exercises, or face up for flexion work. The center line aligns with your spine from the first moment.

2

Find your body zone

Shoulder markers, hip lines, knee guides — each zone tells you exactly where that body part should start for each exercise. No measuring, no estimating.

3

Use the angle guides to move

As you perform each movement, the angle lines give you a constant visual reference. You can see — in real time — whether you're staying within the correct range.

4

Track consistency with the ruler marks

The lateral ruler marks let you reproduce the same exercise with the same range of motion every session. This is how progressive improvement becomes measurable.

5

Follow the routine — daily

Consistency is the key to lasting lower back relief. A 10–15 minute daily practice with correct alignment produces results that sporadic, unguided exercise cannot.

Why guided movement

Pilates-based exercises reduce pain

A 2022 review of 118 controlled trials found structured, guided movement exercises had the highest likelihood of reducing back pain and disability among all intervention types studied.

Research shows that the vast majority of disc herniations resolve with consistent, correctly performed movement therapy — the key word being correctly.
