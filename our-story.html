Chapter 01

The diagnosis that changed everything

Five years ago, I received a diagnosis that redefined how I thought about my body: a herniated disc. Like most people in that moment, my first instinct was to stop moving. Rest. Wait for it to get better on its own.

It didn't. And after weeks of pain, restricted movement, and frustrated attempts at home exercises that felt uncertain and unsafe, I turned to physical therapy.

"The exercises worked. But only when I did them correctly. And correctly, without visual guidance, was nearly impossible to achieve at home."

My therapist gave me a list of exercises. What they couldn't give me was the ability to replicate their hands-on guidance in my own living room. Every session at home felt like guesswork — am I rotated the right way? Is my hip in the right position? How far is too far?

Chapter 02

Designing a solution for myself

I started marking my own mat. First with tape. Then with more precision — circles for my center point, lines for alignment references, zones for body placement. Within weeks, something changed.

The exercises became unambiguous. I stopped second-guessing my positioning. My routine became consistent. And for the first time since the diagnosis, I felt real, lasting progress.

Over the following years, I refined the system — working through dozens of iterations of the marking layout, refining which reference points actually mattered for the exercises that delivered results. The ones rooted in evidence. The ones physical therapists actually recommend.

"What I had was not just a marked-up mat. It was a guided movement system — and it was working."
Chapter 03

The decision to share it

For years, ALIGNA was a personal tool. It wasn't until I began talking to others dealing with lower back pain — colleagues, friends, family — that I realised how universal the problem was.

Most people knew they should exercise. Most had tried. Most had stopped — not because they lacked motivation, but because they lacked a system they trusted. A reference. Something that told them: this is right, this is where you should be, this is how you move safely.

That's when I decided to produce ALIGNA properly. To turn five years of personal development into a product that anyone could use — regardless of prior experience, regardless of access to a therapist, regardless of where they live.

This is not just a mat. It is a system that gives your body a framework for healing itself — with structure, consistency, and confidence.

Our mission

To help people feel more confident moving their bodies at home — through guided design, premium quality, and a system built on real experience.

Brand values

Clarity

Every decision — in design, in communication, in product — should reduce confusion, not add to it. The mat's markings exist because clarity enables correct movement.

Simplicity

The most powerful tools are often the simplest ones. We resist complexity for its own sake. What matters is what works — used consistently, used correctly.

Consistency

Results in movement come from showing up daily. We design for the person who wants to build a practice, not just try something once.

Quality

Premium is not about price — it's about intention. Every material choice, every marking decision, every word on this site reflects a commitment to quality over volume.

Access

Professional-quality movement guidance should not require a clinic, a therapist, or an expensive gym. ALIGNA makes that guidance physical, portable, and permanent.

Honesty

We are a wellness product, not a medical solution. We will always be transparent about what ALIGNA can and cannot do — and we will never overstate our claims.
